## 👋 **So... this is how I ended up here**

Picture this: It's 2 AM, I'm helping a local bakery fix their Wi-Fi, and I notice something weird in their network traffic. Like, really weird. Not malicious, just... odd. That rabbit hole led to another, and somehow I ended up as "that security guy" in Peine.

Now I spend my nights staring at code, drinking way too much coffee, and finding patterns in digital chaos. This document is basically me trying to explain how I do what I do, in case anyone's curious (or in case I forget myself).

---

## 🎯 **My "Methodology" (fancy word for "how I stumble onto stuff")**

### **Step 1: The "Huh, That's Weird" Moment**
Everything starts with something catching my eye:
- A weird error message in a log file
- Network traffic that doesn't make sense
- A customer complaint that defies physics (yes, that actually happened)
- Code that's way too clever for its own good
- Someone mentioning something odd on Reddit

Basically, if it makes me go "huh," I'm probably going to spend the next 48 hours obsessing over it.

### **Step 2: Falling Down the Rabbit Hole**
Once something catches my attention, I can't let it go:

- **Google Fu**: I become a digital detective, searching every corner of the internet
- **Code Diving**: I'll read through source code like it's a mystery novel
- **Network Sniffing**: Packet captures become my bedtime stories
- **Social Engineering**: Sometimes I just ask people stuff (nicely, I swear)
- **Coffee Consumption**: Increases exponentially during this phase

### **Step 3: Documenting Everything (or trying to)**
I have a terrible memory, so I write down everything:
- Screenshots of weird stuff
- Notes that make sense at 3 AM but not the next day
- Code snippets with comments like "WTF is this?"
- Timeline of when things happened
- Random theories that probably sound crazy

### **Step 4: The "Oh!" Moment**
Sometimes, after days of staring at stuff, everything clicks:
- Patterns emerge from chaos
- Weird connections make sense
- The "why" becomes clear
- I feel like a genius for about 5 minutes

### **Step 5: Sharing What I Found**
Because knowledge should be free, and misery loves company:
- Write it all down (badly, but comprehensively)
- Share with the security community
- Help people fix their stuff
- Move on to the next weird thing
